# Primary Literature

Use this file when the user asks for publication-backed claims, protocol grounding, or references to cite/check.

## 1. PIP-seq platform paper

Clark, I. C. et al. `Microfluidics-free single-cell genomics with templated emulsification.` *Nature Biotechnology* (2023).

- DOI: `10.1038/s41587-023-01685-z`
- Use this paper for:
  - what templated emulsification / PIP-seq is
  - the claim that the workflow is microfluidics-free
  - the general structure of barcoded hydrogel-templated partitioning
  - platform-scale and workflow framing

Do not use this paper alone to invent Wolffia-specific wet-lab parameters.

## 2. Wolffia transcriptomic / preparation benchmark

Wu, Y. et al. `Streamlined spatial and environmental expression signatures characterize the minimalist duckweed Wolffia australiana.` *Genome Research* 34(7):1106-1122 (2024).

- DOI: `10.1101/gr.279091.124`
- Project link used earlier: `https://genome.cshlp.org/content/34/7/1106`
- Use this paper for:
  - Wolffia culture conditions used as a benchmark
  - pooled-plant/frond handling language
  - rationale for razor dicing because of the waterproof cuticle
  - the benchmark protoplast digestion chemistry and wash logic

## 3. Wolffia genome / biological context

Park, J. et al. `Genome of the world's smallest flowering plant, Wolffia australiana, helps explain its specialized physiology and unique morphology.` *Communications Biology* (2021).

- DOI: `10.1038/s42003-021-02389-w`
- Use this paper for:
  - broad Wolffia biological context
  - morphology / reduction framing

Hoang, P. T. N. et al. `The genome of Wolffia australiana facilitates discovery of genetic basis for aquatic adaptation in duckweeds.` *The Plant Cell* (2022).

- DOI: `10.1093/plcell/koac068`
- Use this paper for:
  - aquatic adaptation framing
  - Wolffia genomic context

## Citation Rule

When writing outward-facing summaries or protocols:

- cite the PIP-seq platform paper for platform mechanics
- cite the 2024 Wolffia paper for Wolffia-specific prep/culture logic
- cite the genome papers for biological context, not for dissociation or partitioning mechanics

## Check Before Finalizing

Before presenting a scientific claim as settled, verify:

1. Is it directly supported by one of the papers above?
2. Is it instead a repository convention or working assumption?
3. Does it still need confirmation from the lab, kit provider, or sequencing core?
